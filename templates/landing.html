why we built this

Kids lose the script long before they lose the language

Growing up outside the country where it's spoken, a child can often speak a little and understand more — but never quite learn to read it. School doesn't teach it. There isn't always a Saturday class nearby, or one for every language a family actually needs at home. My Mom's Stories is a small, daily way to keep the script alive — letter by letter, word by word, story by story.

the name

Every language calls it the same thing

Bengali calls it মাতৃভাষা. Hindi calls it मातृभाषा. Arabic calls it اللغة الأم. Different scripts, the same idea — the language you first heard from your mother, not the one you were taught in a classroom. My Mom's Stories starts there: the stories she already tells, written down in the script your child is learning to read.

questions parents ask

Frequently asked

Which languages does My Mom's Stories teach?

Bengali, Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Arabic, Spanish, Japanese, Russian, Ukrainian and Romanian to start, each in its real script. More are being added — tell us which one you need.

Does my child need to already speak the language?

No. Most children already understand or speak some of it at home. This teaches reading — alphabet, pronunciation, small words, small lines, and two well-known stories in the script.

How much does it cost?

£15 a month includes 15 hours of access. Extra time beyond that is billed at £1.50 an hour.

Can I see what my child has done?

Yes — every letter read, every word attempted and every minute spent is visible on the parent dashboard. You can clear your child's history completely, any time.

Do I need to know the language myself?

No. Your child logs in with their own nickname and password and works through lessons independently — you just pick the country and language at sign-up.
